What is Artificial Intelligence?

AI is a technology that allows machines to think, learn, reason, solve problems and make decisions like humans.

Core Technologies: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Natural Language Processing (NLP).

History of AI

1950

Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test to check if a machine can think like a human.

1956

John McCarthy coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference. He is known as the Father of AI.

1960s – 1970s

Initial research grew rapidly, but hit limitations (AI Winter) due to hardware limits.

1980s – Present

Returned via Expert Systems, and boomed with modern Machine Learning & Deep Learning.

Types of AI

1. Narrow AI

Can perform only a specific task. Examples: Siri, Google Assistant, ChatGPT.

2. General AI

Not fully developed yet. It will have the ability to perform intellectual tasks like a human.

3. Super AI

A purely theoretical concept where machines would exceed human intelligence.

Advantages of AI

  • AI can work exceptionally fast and makes fewer mistakes.
  • It operates 24 hours, 7 days a week flawlessly without fatigue.
  • It quickly extracts value from huge amounts of raw data.
  • Robots can substitute humans in highly dangerous work.
Slide 6 of 10
7

Disadvantages of AI

  • Certain job sectors may face critical reductions.
  • AI development and infrastructure maintenance require high costs.
  • AI models cannot genuinely understand deep human emotions.
  • It can generate incorrect outputs if trained on wrong datasets.
